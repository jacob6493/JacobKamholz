About
Clinician-Researcher

Jacob C. Kamholz is a PhD student in Health Services Management & Policy at The Ohio State University College of Public Health and an EMS Research Fellow at the National Registry of Emergency Medical Technicians. With over a decade of frontline EMS experience spanning Pennsylvania, Colorado, New York, and Ohio, his work bridges clinical reality with the systemic policy challenges that shape the EMS workforce.

His research focuses on EMS workforce policy, education outcomes, clinician retention, emergency preparedness, and clinician preparedness. He also works as a paramedic at OSU Wexner Medical Center and as an occupational health paramedic with Amphibious Medics/TerraSmart.

Research Focus
Areas of Inquiry
01

EMS Workforce Turnover & Retention

Investigating organizational culture, job satisfaction, and structural drivers of clinician attrition in emergency medical services.

02

Education Outcomes & Certification

Evaluating EMS education program performance, attrition, pass rates, and the role of medical director involvement.

03

Revenue Capture & Reimbursement

Examining how transport-based reimbursement models shape operations, financial sustainability, and access to care.

04

Emergency & Behavioral Health Preparedness

Community disaster planning, behavioral health readiness, and how preparedness influences clinician retention.

05

Geographic & Operational Disparities

Spatial variations in EMS service availability and policy implications for rural and underserved communities.

06

Clinician Advocacy & Profession Promotion

Understanding how likely EMS clinicians are to recommend their profession, and what shapes that willingness.
